Revision

For both cations and anions:

  1. What charge carrier is important here?
  2. Has the atom gained or lost some of that charge carrier?
  3. What is the overall charge of ion?
  1. If Max rubs a balloon on his head and it gains a charge of \(2.5C\), how many electrons has it lost?
  2. What process is causing the balloon to lose electrons and Max to gain them?

\[\begin{align*} & E = \frac{V}{d} \end{align*}\]

\[\begin{align*} & \vec{F} = \vec{E}q \\ \end{align*}\]

  1. Draw a uniform electric field between two plates with a potential difference of \(2,000V\) and distance of \(25cm\).
  2. An object with charge \(-0.75C\) is placed in the field. Calculate the force that will be exerted on it by the electric field.

\[\begin{align*} & I = \frac{q}{t} \end{align*}\]

  1. A current of \(7A\) is flowing around a circuit. How many electrons are passing a point each second?
  2. How many electrons pass the same point in one hour?

\[\begin{align*} & P = IV \\ & V = IR \end{align*}\]

  1. What is the resistance of a \(6V\) bulb with \(3A\) passing through it?
  2. What is the power of a \(12V\) bulb with a resistance of \(0.2\Omega\)?
